Weather Knowledge Center

Learn the fundamental meteorological principles driving our climate and analytics engines.

Relative Humidity

The ratio of water vapor in the air compared to the maximum amount the air can hold at that specific temperature. Higher humidity makes hot days feel sticky because sweat cannot evaporate quickly.

Wind Dynamics

Wind is caused by differences in atmospheric pressure. Air moves from high-pressure zones to low-pressure zones. The steeper the pressure gradient, the higher the wind speed.

Climate vs Weather

Weather describes short-term atmospheric conditions (minutes to weeks), like a rainy afternoon. Climate represents the long-term patterns and averages of weather over 30+ years.

Heat Index

Also known as "apparent temperature", it combines actual air temperature and relative humidity to measure how hot it actually feels to the human body.
